A stone stall in Cebu City, Philippines, is known as the Magellan's Cross Pavilion. The building, which is located on Plaza Sugbo next to the Basilica del Santo Nio, houses a Christian cross that was planted there on April 21, 1521, by members of the Spanish expedition of Ferdinand Magellan's historic first voyage around the globe. The pavilion is listed as a National Cultural Treasure of the Philippines, along with the church and convent structures at the Basilica del Santo Nio.
2 / 3
Bohol is a province in the Central Visayas area of the Philippines. It consists of Bohol Island and a large number of neighboring smaller islands. Coral reefs and extraordinary geological formations, including the Chocolate Hills, are highlights of Bohol. These around 1,200 symmetrical mounds on the main island, close to the town of Carmen, turn cocoa-brown during the dry season, standing out against the lush jungle surroundings.
